What is an electronic signature

An electronic signature is an electronic signature or processing system that takes electronic form, and is attached to or linked to an electronic message with the intention of authenticating or approving the message and enables companies to secure data, transactions and documents and provides evidence that data has not been tampered with.
Anyone allowed to sign (on behalf of a company or on their own) can obtain a security token application device with an electronic signature certificate.
Electronic signature capabilities allow citizens and organizations to conduct government transactions remotely or increase security services within their organizations.

What is an electronic seal

An electronic seal is an electronic form (seal) used to ensure the authenticity and origin of data within a document issued to an entity or person authorized to act on behalf of a legal entity.
It is an electronic signature that enables organizations to secure any type of digital asset, whether it is data, transactions or documents, the electronic seal guarantees authenticity and integrity.
The electronic seal is the modern and richer counterpart to the traditional company seal and provides evidence that the data or system has not been tampered with.

Uses of Electronic Seal

  • The electronic stamp must be used for any company dealing with the Egyptian Tax Authority and using the electronic invoice system.
  • It can be used to quickly sign a large amount of documents on behalf of your company (such as invoices or statements), as well as to provide additional confirmation when approving individual reports (such as medical analysis).

What is the difference between an electronic seal and an electronic signature?

  • Electronic Seal:

    • It is linked to the company's registration number and is used in two cases of self-registration and in the case of an ERP system with the financier either

  • Electronic Signature:

    • It is linked to the national number of the delegate and is used in one case only, which is the use of the portal, and therefore it is not suitable for self-registration
